# Philosophy

Sarthak is built for **augmentation**, not automation. The goal is to amplify what humans can do — not to replace human judgment.

## Principles

**Delegate the mechanical.** Background capture, routine aggregation, pattern detection, workspace organization — these are cognitively expensive but not meaningful. They belong to code.

**Keep meaning human.** Decisions, interpretation, and intent stay with the person. Sarthak surfaces signal; you decide what to do with it.

**Privacy is architecture, not a feature.** Data stays on the machine. Encryption happens before storage. Images are never written to disk. The system is designed so that even if you use a cloud AI provider, your raw activity never leaves your machine.

**Understanding over memorization.** Sarthak Spaces builds genuine mastery — math first, then code, then projects. The goal is to understand why something works, not just how to use it.

**Consistency beats intensity.** A 5-minute daily session compounds faster than a weekend marathon. The quick-win system and streak tracking exist for this reason.

## What this means in practice

The capture system records what is cognitively impossible to track at scale — context switches, command patterns, time distributions — so you can act on the summary, not reconstruct it.

The Spaces mastery engine selects the next concept at the exact edge of your current ability (Zone of Proximal Development), provides the mathematical foundation, and gives you a task that makes the concept real. It adapts to your background — a doctor gets clinical analogies; an engineer gets systems analogies.

The workspace transformer reshapes your directory to mirror how an expert organizes their work. The README files contain real expert tips, not generic descriptions.

## The bigger picture

Every person who uses Sarthak Spaces should leave with a workspace, a roadmap, and a set of skills that look like those of a practitioner in their field. Whether they are preparing for an exam, learning a new domain, or building a career — the tools, structure, and personalized teaching should be indistinguishable from having a senior mentor available every day.

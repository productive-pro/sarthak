# Sarthak Spaces

Sarthak Spaces is a structured learning environment that adapts entirely to who you are. Tell it your background and goal, and it builds a personalised curriculum, tracks your progress, and teaches each concept in a way that makes sense for you.

Everything described here is available in the web UI under **Spaces**. The [Web UI guide](web.md) covers the full interface in detail.

---

## Creating a Space

Open the **Spaces** section and click **+ New Space**. Fill in your workspace folder, domain type, background, and learning goal. Sarthak generates a full AI curriculum roadmap and sets up the folder structure.

Supported domains: Data Science, AI Engineering, Software Engineering, Medicine, Education, Exam Prep, Research, and Custom.

---

## How Sarthak teaches you

Sarthak always picks the next concept at the edge of what you already know — not too easy, not too far ahead. Concepts you are struggling with come back sooner; concepts you have mastered come back on a spaced repetition schedule so you do not forget them.

Every explanation is adapted to your background. If you are a doctor, Sarthak uses medical analogies. If you are a business analyst, it avoids unnecessary code. If you want deeper maths, just ask.

---

## Progression and XP

As you complete sessions and tests, you earn XP and level up.

| Level | XP |
|:---|:---|
| Novice | 0 |
| Beginner | 100 |
| Intermediate | 300 |
| Advanced | 700 |
| Expert | 1500 |

Your streak tracks how many consecutive days you have done at least one learning session. Your level, XP, streak, and mastered concepts are visible on the Dashboard and in every Space card.

## The roadmap board

Your curriculum is organised into chapters, topics, and concepts on a Kanban board with four columns: **Not Started**, **In Progress**, **Review**, and **Completed**.

Drag a chapter card to a new column to update its status. Click into a chapter to see its topics; click into a topic to work on concepts. The AI can generate topics and concepts for you, or you can add and organise them yourself.

---

## Working inside a concept

Each concept has a full workspace with six tabs:

**Notes** — write in Markdown, drop in a PDF or Word document to convert it, or dictate using speech-to-text. Notes are versioned and saved automatically.

**Explains** — ask Sarthak to explain this concept, get a summary, or ask a specific question. Answers are grounded in your own notes when available.

**QuickTest** — take a short timed test on this concept and get immediate feedback.

**Record** — record audio or video and get an automatic transcript added to your notes.

**Notebook** — a live code notebook for experimenting.

**Playground** — an interactive sandbox for this concept.

---

## Space panels

From the Space home, the toolbar buttons open side panels for deeper tools:

**SRS (Spaced Repetition)** — shows every concept due for review today, ranked by how urgently you need to revisit it.

**Practice** — run a full practice test drawn from your roadmap or your own notes and files. After the test, Sarthak updates your review schedule and surfaces weak areas.

**Insights** — your session analytics: focus scores, understanding trends, and what Sarthak recommends you work on next.

**Knowledge Graph** — an interactive map of how your concepts connect to each other.

**Digest** — an AI-generated narrative summary of your recent learning with suggested next steps.

**Notes** — all notes across the Space in one searchable view.

**Tasks** — a to-do manager tied to this Space.

**Workspace** — browse the files inside your Space folder.

---

## Document search (RAG)

If you enabled document search when creating the Space, Sarthak indexes your notes, PDFs, and code files and uses them as context during sessions, explanations, and practice tests. The index updates automatically when you add or change files.

---

## For non-technical learners

If your background is non-technical, Sarthak adapts without you needing to do anything:

- Every concept starts with why it matters in your field before any technical content
- Analogies come from your domain (medical, business, education, etc.)
- Tasks have a no-code version using spreadsheets or interactive tools
- Mathematical notation is kept minimal unless you ask for more depth
